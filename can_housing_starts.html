Canadian Housing Starts¶

StatsCan regularly collects information on new housing construction in Canada, something that has become ever more important as housing demand has increased and the population of Canada's largest cities has greatly increased. We'll grab the data on various categories of housing in Canada and also data on Canadian GDP as well, something that should correlate with housing starts.

Clearly the economic turmoil and more restrictive lending conditions following during the recession were a noticeable setback for the Canadian housing industry's apparently constant upward trend. To further investigate the the upward trend of Canada's housing construction we can investigate the relationship between housing construction and Canada's GDP.

















Our first step is to split our data and attempt to generate regressions to see how well we can predict against our known set.

It appears that GDP alone is a poor predictor of total housing starts, its ability to predict housing starts may also be influenced by it having a lagging effect on housing starts rather than an effect in the same month.


It is also likely that we need to add additional features such as population, the Bank of Canada's prime rate, and the general availability of credit to consumers.


Going forward it would also be useful to generate seasonal or quarterly dummy variables and use them to quantify the effect winter has on housing construction.
